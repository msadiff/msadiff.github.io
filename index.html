Abstract

Cross-modal understanding and generation, particularly in video-to-audio tasks, have seen significant progress with models leveraging contrastive learning to align video and audio embeddings. Prior works employ contrastive pretraining to improve modality alignment, while diffusion-based generative models have demonstrated promising results in synthesizing high-quality audio from visual inputs. However, existing methods often struggle to capture the fine-grained multi-scale spatial-temporal dependencies required for effective cross-modal understanding and high-fidelity audio generation. To address this, we introduce MSADiff, a novel framework that unifies contrastive learning with multi-scale generative modeling for cross-modal pretraining. MSADiff incorporates a multi-scale spatial-temporal alignment mechanism to enhance video-audio correspondence and leverages a diffusion-based decoder conditioned on multi-scale video representations for high-quality audio synthesis. By integrating discriminative and generative objectives, our approach ensures robust cross-modal alignment while improving the fidelity and coherence of generated audio. Extensive experiments on VGGSound and AudioSet demonstrate that MSADiff outperforms state-of-the-art methods in video-to-audio generation and retrieval.
